Essentials of a Great Resume

Learn what recruiters look for and how to make your resume stand out from thousands of others.

Keep it concise

Fresh graduates should keep resume to 1 page. Experienced professionals can go up to 2 pages. Recruiters spend only 6-7 seconds on first scan.

ATS Friendly Format

Most companies use ATS software to filter resumes. Use simple fonts, avoid tables/images in text, and include keywords from the job description.

Quantify Achievements

Instead of "improved performance", write "improved performance by 40%". Numbers make your achievements concrete and impressive to recruiters.

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong action verbs like "Developed", "Led", "Implemented", "Optimized", "Designed". Avoid passive language like "was responsible for".

Consistent Formatting

Use the same font, size, and spacing throughout. Pick one professional font (Arial, Calibri, Georgia). Keep margins at 0.5-1 inch for clean presentation.

Tailor for Each Job

Customize your resume for each application. Mirror keywords from the job description. A tailored resume is 3x more likely to pass ATS screening.

Do This

Include a strong career objective or professional summary at the top
List work experience in reverse chronological order (latest first)
Include relevant keywords from the job description
Proofread carefully — even one typo can cost you the interview
Use consistent date formats (e.g. Jan 2024 – Present)
Include your LinkedIn, GitHub, or portfolio links
Save and send as PDF to preserve formatting

Avoid This

Don't use unprofessional email addresses (e.g. coolboy123@gmail.com)
Don't include personal info like age, religion, or marital status
Don't use the same generic resume for every application
Don't use fancy graphics or charts — ATS can't read them
Don't write "References available on request" — waste of space
Don't lie or exaggerate — background checks will catch it
Don't use very small fonts — minimum 10-11pt for body text

Understanding ATS (Applicant Tracking System)

Over 75% of companies use ATS to filter resumes before a human sees them. Here's how to beat it.

Use Exact Keywords

Copy exact phrases from the job description. If they say "project management", don't write "managing projects". ATS looks for exact matches.

Submit as PDF or DOCX

Always submit as PDF unless DOCX is specifically requested. PDF preserves your formatting and is readable by modern ATS systems.

Simple Fonts Only

Stick to Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, or Georgia. Fancy decorative fonts may not be parsed correctly by ATS software.

Standard Section Headers

Use standard headers like "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills". Creative names like "My Journey" confuse ATS and may not be recognized.

What Sections to Include

Prioritize these sections based on your experience level and the job you're applying for.

1

Contact Information

Name, email, phone, city, LinkedIn. Always at the top.

2

Career Objective / Summary

2-3 lines summarizing who you are and what you bring.

3

Work Experience

Most important section. List in reverse chronological order.

4

Education

Degree, college name, graduation year, CGPA if above 7.

5

Skills

Technical and soft skills relevant to the job. Be specific.

6

Projects

Crucial for freshers. Show what you can build in practice.

7

Certifications

Online courses, industry certifications. Add if relevant.

8

Achievements

Awards, hackathons, competitive rankings, publications.

9

Languages / Interests

Optional. Add only if relevant or space permits.
